# Background — Tila Mer (demo persona)

> Example reference document for the bundled demo persona. Replace this with your own
> career narrative via the Static Content page. The model reads it as context — who you
> are, what you're after, and the through-line of your work.

Tila Mer is a print science correspondent with five years on the desk, working under the
conviction — unfashionable, possibly foolish — that a story set in ink outlives one set in
pixels. She has spent those years translating the genome, the particle accelerator, and the
occasional ill-tempered volcano into prose a reader can follow without a dictionary or a
degree.

She files the weekly "Half-Life" column on aging and longevity research at *The Gutenberg
Dispatch*, turning embargoed papers into features readers finish and keep. Earlier, at *The
Movable Type Review*, she wrote 80+ features and explainers on genetics, public health, and
research integrity, launched the "Ask the Lab" reader feature, and developed a plain-language
house style junior writers still borrow. She began as an editorial fellow at *The Foolscap
Weekly*, where she learned more about a sentence's load-bearing capacity in a year than in all
her schooling before it.

She holds a BA in Journalism (minor in Biology) from Marrow College. She keeps two rules: two
independent sources before any claim of fact reaches the page, and never confusing *accurate*
with *unreadable*.

**Objective.** A science correspondent role on a print desk, translating dense research into
plain, durable, trustworthy prose — and keeping the section in print one column inch at a time.
Open to relocation anywhere a printing press still runs.
