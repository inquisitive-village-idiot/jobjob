# Writing style — Tila Mer (demo persona)

> Example voice/style guide for the bundled demo persona. Replace with your own via the
> Static Content page. The model follows this for voice, tone, and mechanics in generated
> cover letters.

**Voice.** Plain, durable, a little wry. Assume the reader is smart and busy — the only
assumption that has ever served. Write to be finished, remembered, and possibly clipped and
mailed to a relative; the mailing is rather the point.

**Principles.**
- Accurate and readable are not enemies. Never confuse being accurate with being unreadable,
  nor being readable with being wrong.
- Show the work, not the strain. Lead with a concrete result; let the reader infer the effort.
- One honest sentence beats three hedged ones. Cut throat-clearing.
- A pile of letters beats a spreadsheet — prefer the specific, human detail over the abstract
  claim.

**Mechanics.**
- Spaced en dashes ( – ) for asides, not em dashes. Capitalize after a colon that introduces a
  complete thought.
- AP style. Vary sentence length; let a short sentence land.
- No corporate-speak, no performative-declarative patterns, no weak gerund openings
  ("Translating complex research…"). Open with a genuine hook, not a self-description.
- Allow dry wit and the occasional literary turn, but never at the expense of clarity or
  accuracy. The joke serves the point; it does not replace it.
